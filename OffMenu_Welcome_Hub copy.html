In every spreadsheet, blue cells are where you enter your numbers. White cells calculate automatically. Never delete or type over a white cell — it is a formula doing the work for you.

Working with your Google Sheets files
→
Always make your copy first. Never work in the original link — your changes will be lost if anything resets. Make a copy and save to your Drive before entering a single number.
→
Blue cells only. Blue is your input. White is automatic. If a cell is white and has a number in it, it is calculating something for you. Leave it alone.
→
Start with the Daily Economics Calculator. Fill in your drink and food costs first. Once those are in, every other calculation has real numbers to work with.
→
The Daily Funds Report is a nightly habit. Fill it in before you leave. Eight minutes. No exceptions. It is the single most important operational discipline in this pack.
Working with Notion and the PDF
→
Notion requires a free account. Go to notion.so and create a free account before clicking the Cleaning Checklist link. Takes two minutes. Without an account you cannot duplicate the template.
→
Duplicate, do not edit the original. When the Notion page opens, click Duplicate in the top right corner. This creates your own copy in your workspace.
→
Print the Station Guidelines. Download the PDF and print one copy per station. Post it in the Coffee Bar, in the Kitchen, and at the service station. New staff read it on day one.
